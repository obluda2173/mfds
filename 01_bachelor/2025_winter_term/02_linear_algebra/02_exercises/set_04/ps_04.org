* Problem 10 (a) :DONE:
** Task
#+begin_quote
In this exercise, we work over the field $\mathbb{R}$. Consider
$A=\begin{bmatrix}2&1&-1\\ 4&5&-3\\ -2&5&-2\end{bmatrix}\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times3}$,
$b=\begin{bmatrix}1\\ 7\\ 3\end{bmatrix}\in\mathbb{R}^{3}$.
Perform Gaussian elimination on the system $Ax=b$ without row exchanges. Show explicitly how you eliminate the entries below the diagonal in the first and second columns, and write down the resulting upper-triangular matrix $U$.
#+end_quote

** Solution

\begin{align*}
        \begin{bmatrix}
                2&1&-1\\
                4&5&-3\\
                -2&5&-2
        \end{bmatrix}
        \times
        \begin{bmatrix}
                x_{1}\\
                x_{2}\\
                x_{3}
        \end{bmatrix}
        =
        \begin{bmatrix}
                1\\
                7\\
                3
        \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}

$$[A \mid b] =
\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
2 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\
4 & 5 & -3 & 7 \\
-2 & 5 & -2 & 3
\end{array}\right]$$

- *Step 1: Eliminate Column 1*
  *Pivot*: $a_{11} = 2$.

  - *Row 2 Operation*: $R_{2} \leftarrow R_{2} - (2) \times R_{1}$

    $$\begin{aligned}
        \text{Row 2: } & \left[ 4, 5, -3 \mid 7 \right] \\
        -(2) \times \text{Row 1: } & \left[ -4, -2, 2 \mid -2 \right] \\
        \hline
        \text{New Row 2: } & [0, 3, -1 \mid 5] \\
    \end{aligned}$$

  - *Row 3 Operation*: $R_{3} \leftarrow R_{3} - (-1) \times R_{1}$

    $$\begin{aligned}
        \text{Row 3: } & \left[ -2, 5, -2 \mid 3 \right] \\
        -(-1)\text{Row 1: } & \left[ 2, 1, -1 \mid 1 \right] \\
        \hline
        \text{New Row 3: } & [0, 6, -3 \mid 4] \\
    \end{aligned}$$

*Current Augmented Matrix*:

$$\begin{align*}
        \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
        2 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\
        0 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\
        0 & 6 & -3 &4
        \end{array}\right]
\end{align*}$$


- *Step 2: Eliminate Column 2*
  *Pivot*: $a_{22} = 3$.

  - *Row 3 Operation*: $R_{3} \leftarrow R_{3} - (2) \times R_{2}$

    $$\begin{aligned}
        \text{Row 3: } & \left[ 0, 6, -3 \mid 4 \right] \\
        -(2) \times \text{Row 2: } & \left[ 0, -6, 2 \mid -10 \right] \\
        \hline
        \text{New Row 3: } & [0, 0, -1 \mid -6] \\
    \end{aligned}$$

*Final Upper Triangular Form*:

$$\begin{align*}
        \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
        2 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\
        0 & 3 & -1 & 5 \\
        0 & 0 & -1 & -6
        \end{array}\right]
\end{align*}$$

*Upper Triangular Matrix U*:

$$U = \begin{bmatrix}
        2 & 1 & -1 \\
        0 & 3 & -1 \\
        0 & 0 & -1
      \end{bmatrix}$$

#+begin_quote
Let $m_{21}$, $m_{31}$ be the multipliers used to eliminate the entries in the first column, and let $m_{32}$ be the multiplier used to eliminate the (3, 2)-entry after the first elimination step.
Form the unit lower-triangular matrix
$L=\begin{bmatrix}1&0&0\\ m_{21}&1&0\\ m_{31}&m_{32}&1\end{bmatrix}$
Verify that your matrices satisfy $A=LU$.
#+end_quote

** Solution

From the row operations in Part (a), we identify the multipliers:
$$ m_{21} = 2, \quad m_{31} = -1, \quad m_{32} = 2 $$

Constructing $L$:
$$
L = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
m_{21} & 1 & 0 \\
m_{31} & m_{32} & 1
\end{bmatrix}
= \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
2 & 1 & 0 \\
-1 & 2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

We verify the decomposition by computing the product $LU$:


\begin{aligned}
LU &= \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
2 & 1 & 0 \\
-1 & 2 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 1 & -1 \\
0 & 3 & -1 \\
0 & 0 & -1
\end{bmatrix} \\
&= \begin{bmatrix}
(1)(2) & (1)(1) & (1)(-1) \\
(2)(2) & (2)(1) + (1)(3) & (2)(-1) + (1)(-1) \\
(-1)(2) & (-1)(1) + (2)(3) & (-1)(-1) + (2)(-1) + (1)(-1)
\end{bmatrix} \\
&= \begin{bmatrix}
2 & 1 & -1 \\
4 & 5 & -3 \\
-2 & 5 & -2
\end{bmatrix} \\
&= A \quad \checkmark
\end{aligned}

* Problem 10 (c) :DONE:
** Task
#+begin_quote
Solve $Ax=b$ using the factorization $A=LU$ by (i) solving $Ly=b$ for $y$ (forward substitution) and then (ii) solving $Ux=y$ for $x$ (backward substitution). Present your intermediate vector $y$ and the final solution $x$.
#+end_quote

** Solution

We solve $Ax=b$ by using the decomposition $A=LU$. This allows us to solve two triangular systems:
\begin{align*}
Ly &= b \quad \text{(where }y = Ux \text{)} \\
Ux &= y
\end{align*}

- *(i) Forward Substitution* ($Ly=b$)

  We set up the system:

  \begin{align*}
        \begin{bmatrix}
                1  & 0 & 0 \\
                2  & 1 & 0 \\
                -1 & 2 & 1
        \end{bmatrix}
        \begin{bmatrix}
                y_1 \\
                y_2 \\
                y_3
        \end{bmatrix}
        &=
        \begin{bmatrix}
                1 \\
                7 \\
                3
        \end{bmatrix} \\
  \end{align*}

  From the first row:
  $$ y_1 = 1 $$

  From the second row:
  $$ 2y_1 + y_2 = 7 \implies 2(1) + y_2 = 7 \implies y_2 = 5 $$

  From the third row:
  $$ -1y_1 + 2y_2 + y_3 = 3 \implies -1(1) + 2(5) + y_3 = 3 \implies 9 + y_3 = 3 \implies y_3 = -6 $$

  The intermediate vector is:
  $$ y = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix} $$

- *(ii) Backward Substitution* ($Ux=y$)
  We now solve for $x$:

  \begin{align*}
        \begin{bmatrix}
                2 & 1 & -1 \\
                0 & 3 & -1 \\
                0 & 0 & -1
        \end{bmatrix}
        \begin{bmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{bmatrix}
        &=
        \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix} \
  \end{align*}

  From the third row:
  $$ -1x_3 = -6 \implies x_3 = 6 $$

  From the second row:
  $$ 3x_2 - x_3 = 5 \implies 3x_2 - 6 = 5 \implies 3x_2 = 11 \implies x_2 = \frac{11}{3} $$

  From the first row:
  $$ 2x_1 + x_2 - x_3 = 1 \implies 2x_1 + \frac{11}{3} - 6 = 1 $$
  $$ 2x_1 - \frac{7}{3} = 1 \implies 2x_1 = \frac{10}{3} \implies x_1 = \frac{5}{3} $$

  *Final Solution*
  $$
  x = \begin{bmatrix} 5/3 \\ 11/3 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}
  $$

#+begin_src julia :results output :exports both :tangle julia/p_10_d.jl
is_compatible(m_1, m_2) = size(m_1, 2) == size(m_2, 1)

multiply(row, column) = row' * column

function forward_substitution(L, b)
    if !is_compatible(L, b)
        error("Matrix Dimensions Mismatch.")
    end

    n = size(L, 1)

    x = zeros(Float64, n)

    for i in 1:n
        sum_val = b[i]
        for j in 1:(i-1)
            sum_val -= L[i, j] * x[j]
        end
        x[i] = sum_val / L[i, i]
    end
    return x
end

function backward_substitution(U, y)
    if !is_compatible(U, y)
        error("Matrix Dimensions Mismatch.")
    end

    n = size(U, 1)

    x = zeros(Float64, n)

    for i in n:-1:1
        sum_val = y[i]
        for j in (i+1):n
            sum_val -= U[i, j] * x[j]
        end
        x[i] = sum_val / U[i, i]
    end
    return x
end

L = [ 1  0  0;
      2  1  0;
     -1  2  1]

U = [ 2  1 -1;
      0  3 -1;
      0  0 -1]

b = [ 1;
      7;
      3]

y = forward_substitution(L, b)
x = backward_substitution(U, y)

println("Intermediate vector y: ", y)
println("Final solution x: ", x)
#+end_src
