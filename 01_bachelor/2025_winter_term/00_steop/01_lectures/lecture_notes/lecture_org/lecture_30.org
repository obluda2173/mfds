* Introduction and Motivation
** Solvability of Equations
Different number systems allow for the solution of different types of equations.
- The equation $x^2 + 5 = 0$ has no solution in $\mathbb{R}$.
- The equation $\frac{2}{x} = 3$ has a solution in $\mathbb{Q}$.
- The equation $x^2 = 2$ has no solution in $\mathbb{Q}$, but it has a solution in $\mathbb{R}$.

** Cardano's Formula
Consider the cubic equation $y^3 + py + q = 0$.
The roots are given by the formula:
$$ y = \sqrt[3]{-\frac{q}{2} + \sqrt{R}} + \sqrt[3]{-\frac{q}{2} - \sqrt{R}} $$
where $R = (\frac{p}{3})^3 + (\frac{q}{2})^2$.

*** Example
Solve $y^3 - 7y + 6 = 0$.
Here $p = -7$ and $q = 6$.
$$ R = \left(-\frac{7}{3}\right)^3 + 3^2 < 0 $$
Despite $R < 0$, the equation has real solutions: $y_1 = 1, y_2 = 2, y_3 = -3$.
This paradox (roots of negative numbers yielding real solutions) necessitates the introduction of complex numbers.

** Definition
Let $i$ be a symbol such that $i^2 = -1$.
A complex number $z$ is defined as $z = a + bi$ for $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$.
- $\text{Re } z = a$ (Real part of $z$)
- $\text{Im } z = b$ (Imaginary part of $z$)
- $|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$ (Modulus of $z$)
- $\bar{z} = a - bi$ (Complex conjugate)

*** Example Calculation
Solve $x^2 - 2x + 3 = 0$.
$$ D = 4 - 12 = -8 $$
$$ x_{1,2} = \frac{2 \pm \sqrt{-8}}{2} = \frac{2 \pm 2\sqrt{2}i}{2} = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}i $$

* Operations
Let $z = a + bi$ and $w = c + di$.

** Addition
$$ z + w = (a + c) + (b + d)i $$

** Multiplication
$$ z \cdot w = (a + bi)(c + di) = ac + adi + bci + bdi^2 $$
$$ = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i $$

** Division
$$ \frac{z}{w} = \frac{a + bi}{c + di} = \frac{(a + bi)(c - di)}{(c + di)(c - di)} $$
$$ = \frac{(ac + bd) + (bc - ad)i}{c^2 + d^2} $$

** Set Cardinality
$\mathbb{C}$ is the set of all complex numbers.
$$ |\mathbb{C}| = |\mathbb{R}^2| = |\mathbb{R}| = \mathfrak{c} $$

* Geometric Representation
Complex numbers can be represented as points in a plane (Argand plane).
- x-axis: Real axis
- y-axis: Imaginary axis

** Polar / Trigonometric Form
Let $z = a + bi$.
- $a = |z| \cos \theta = r \cos \theta$
- $b = |z| \sin \theta = r \sin \theta$
$$ z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta) $$
where $r = |z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$.

** Properties
- $z \cdot \bar{z} = |z|^2$
- $\theta = \arg z$ (Argument, measured counterclockwise).
- Multiplication by $i$ corresponds to a $90^\circ$ rotation.
  Example: $z = (1 + 2i)i = i + 2i^2 = -2 + i$.

* Properties of Modulus
For $z, w \in \mathbb{C}$:
1. $|z \cdot w| = |z| \cdot |w|$
2. $|\frac{z}{w}| = \frac{|z|}{|w|}$
3. $|\text{Re } z| \le |z|$
4. $|\text{Im } z| \le |z|$
5. $|z + w| \le |z| + |w|$ (Triangle Inequality)

** Proofs
*** Proof of (1)
$$ |z \cdot w|^2 = (ac - bd)^2 + (ad + bc)^2 $$
$$ = a^2c^2 - 2abcd + b^2d^2 + a^2d^2 + 2abcd + b^2c^2 $$
$$ = a^2(c^2 + d^2) + b^2(c^2 + d^2) $$
$$ = (a^2 + b^2)(c^2 + d^2) = |z|^2 |w|^2 $$

*** Proof of (5)
We want to show $|z + w| \le |z| + |w|$.
Square both sides:
$$ |z + w|^2 = (a + c)^2 + (b + d)^2 = |z|^2 + |w|^2 + 2(ac + bd) $$
$$ (|z| + |w|)^2 = |z|^2 + |w|^2 + 2|z||w| $$
The inequality holds if:
$$ ac + bd \le |z||w| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\sqrt{c^2 + d^2} $$
Squaring again (Cauchy-Schwarz context):
$$ (ac + bd)^2 \le (a^2 + b^2)(c^2 + d^2) $$
$$ a^2c^2 + 2abcd + b^2d^2 \le a^2c^2 + a^2d^2 + b^2c^2 + b^2d^2 $$
$$ 2abcd \le a^2d^2 + b^2c^2 $$
$$ 0 \le a^2d^2 - 2abcd + b^2c^2 = (ad - bc)^2 $$
This is always true.

* Geometric Problems
** Sets in the Complex Plane
1. $\{ z \in \mathbb{C} : \arg(z) = 0 \}$ (Positive real axis).
2. $\{ z \in \mathbb{C} : |z| \le 1 \}$ (Unit disk centered at origin).
   3. $\{ z \in \mathbb{C} : |z - i| \le 1 \}$ (Disk of radius 1 centered at $i$).

** Geometric Meaning of Inequality
Consider $|z - 1| \le |\arg z|$ for $|z| = 1$.
- $|z - 1|$ is the chord length connecting $1$ and $z$ on the unit circle.
- $|\arg z|$ is the arc length connecting $1$ and $z$ on the unit circle.
- The inequality states that the chord length is less than or equal to the arc length.
