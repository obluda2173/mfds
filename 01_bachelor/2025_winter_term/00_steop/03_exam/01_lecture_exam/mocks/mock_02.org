* Section B: Open Problems

** Problem B1 (Induction)
Prove $$2^n < n!$$ for $$n \geq 4$$.

** Problem B2 (Set Theory)
Prove $$f^{-1}(A \cap B) = f^{-1}(A) \cap f^{-1}(B)$$.

** Problem B3 (Number Theory)
Prove there are no integer solutions for $$15x^2 - 7y^2 = 9$$.

** Problem B4 (Combinatorics)
Solve $$x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + x_4 = 20$$ with constraints:
$$x_1 \geq 2, \quad x_2 \geq 0, \quad x_3 \geq 1, \quad x_4 \geq 3$$

** Problem B5 (Complex Geometry)
Identify and sketch the locus defined by:
$$|z - 2i| = 2|z + i|$$

** Problem B6 (Polynomials)
Given $$P(x) = x^3 + px + q$$, find $$\sum x_i^2$$ (the sum of the squares of the roots) in terms of $p$.

** Problem B7 (Sequences)
Prove that the sequence defined by $$a_{n+1} = \sqrt{2 + a_n}$$ converges to 2.

** Problem B8 (Limits)
Prove $$\lim_{x \to 2} x^2 = 4$$ using the $\varepsilon-\delta$ definition.

** Problem B9 (Vectors)
Decompose the vector $(1,2,3)$ into components parallel and perpendicular to $(1,0,-1)$.

** Problem B10 (Cardinality)
Prove $$|(0,1)| = |[0,1]|$$ using the Cantor-Schröder-Bernstein (CSB) Theorem.
