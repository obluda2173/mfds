* Section A: Multiple Choice
1. Which statement is logically equivalent to $P \Rightarrow Q$?
   A. $Q \Rightarrow P$
   B. $\neg P \lor Q$
   C. $P \land \neg Q$
   D. $\neg Q \Rightarrow P$

2. If $|A| = n$, what is $|\mathcal{P}(A)|$?
   A. $n^2$
   B. $2^n$
   C. $2n$
   D. $n!$

3. An equivalence relation is:
   A. Reflexive, Antisymmetric, Transitive
   B. Reflexive, Symmetric, Transitive
   C. Irreflexive, Symmetric, Transitive
   D. Reflexive, Symmetric, Antitransitive

4. $f: \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}, f(n) = 2n$ is:
   A. Injective only
   B. Surjective only
   C. Bijective
   D. Neither

5. $\gcd(2^{50} - 1, 2^{20} - 1)$ is:
   A. $2^{20} - 1$
   B. $2^{10} - 1$
   C. $2^{5} - 1$
   D. 1

6. Compute $5^{3} \pmod{7}$.
   A. 2
   B. 4
   C. 5
   D. 6

7. In induction, proving $P(1)$ and $P(k) \Rightarrow P(k+2)$ proves $P(n)$ for:
   A. All $n$
   B. Odd $n$
   C. Even $n$
   D. None

8. Arrangements of "BANANA":
   A. $6!$
   B. $6! / (3! 2! 1!)$
   C. $6! / 3!$
   D. $2^6$

9. Coefficient of $x^2 y^3$ in $(x+y)^5$:
   A. 5
   B. 10
   C. 20
   D. 1

10. $|3 + 4i|$:
    A. 7
    B. 5
    C. 25
    D. $\sqrt{7}$

11. $i$ in exponential form:
    A. $e^{i\pi}$
    B. $e^{i\pi/2}$
    C. $e^{-i\pi/2}$
    D. $e^{i2\pi}$

12. If $1+i$ is a root of $P(x) \in \mathbb{R}[x]$, another root is:
    A. $1-i$
    B. $-1+i$
    C. $-1-i$
    D. $i$

13. Sequence $a_n = (-1)^n$:
    A. Converges to 0
    B. Converges to 1
    C. Bounded but Divergent
    D. Unbounded

14. $\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{3n^2 + 5n}{2n^2 - 1}$:
    A. $3/2$
    B. $0$
    C. $\infty$
    D. $5/2$

15. If $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$, vectors are:
    A. Parallel
    B. Orthogonal
    C. Equal
    D. Collinear

16. Which is a Field?
    A. $\mathbb{Z}$
    B. $\mathbb{Z}_6$
    C. $\mathbb{Q}$
    D. $\mathbb{R}^{2}$

17. Injection $A \to B$ and $B \to A$ implies:
    A. $A = B$
    B. $|A| < |B|$
    C. $|A| = |B|$
    D. $A \subseteq B$

18. 13 pigeons, 12 holes. One hole has:
    A. Exactly 1
    B. Exactly 2
    C. At least 2
    D. 0

19. Negation of $\forall x, \exists y, x < y$:
    A. $\exists x, \forall y, x \ge y$
    B. $\forall x, \exists y, x \ge y$
    C. $\exists x, \exists y, x < y$
    D. $\exists x, \forall y, x < y$

20. $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 9$:
    A. Line
    B. Plane

    D. Sphere radius 9

* Section B: Open Problems

** Problem B1
Prove by induction for $n \ge 1$: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} (2i - 1) = n^2$.

** Problem B2
Let $f: \mathbb{R} \setminus \{3\} \to \mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\}$, $f(x) = \frac{2x + 1}{x - 3}$.
1. Prove $f$ is injective.
2. Prove $f$ is surjective.
3. Find $f^{-1}(x)$.

** Problem B3
Prove $x R y \iff 4 \mid (3x + y)$ is an equivalence relation on $\mathbb{Z}$.

** Problem B4
Solve for smallest positive $x$:
$$ \begin{cases} x \equiv 2 \pmod 3 \\ x \equiv 3 \pmod 5 \\ x \equiv 2 \pmod 7 \end{cases} $$

** Problem B5
Committee of 5 from 8 Math, 6 CS profs.
1. Total ways?
2. Ways with at least 1 Math prof?

** Problem B6
Solve $z^3 = -27i$.

** Problem B7
$P(x) \in \mathbb{R}[x]$ has roots $1$ and $2+i$. Find $P(x)$.

** Problem B8
Prove $\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{2n + 1}{n + 3} = 2$ using $\epsilon-N$ definition.

** Problem B9
Equation of plane through $(1,0,0), (0,2,0), (0,0,3)$.

** Problem B10
Prove $(0,1)$ is uncountable.
