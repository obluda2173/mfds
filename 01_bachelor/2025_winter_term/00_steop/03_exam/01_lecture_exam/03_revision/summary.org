* Logic & Proof Techniques
** Implication & Equivalences
- *Standard Implication:* $P \to Q \equiv \neg P \lor Q$.
- *Contrapositive:* $P \to Q \equiv \neg Q \to \neg P$.
  - /Application:/ Used to prove "Convergent $\implies$ Bounded" by showing "Not Bounded $\implies$ Not Convergent".
- *Negation:* $\neg(P \to Q) \equiv P \land \neg Q$.
- *Quantifiers:*
  - Negation of $\forall x \exists y, P(x,y)$ is $\exists x \forall y, \neg P(x,y)$.
  - "Smallest positive real" logical form: $\forall x \exists y (y < x)$ implies no smallest, versus $\exists x \forall y (x \le y)$.

** Induction Skeletons
- *Standard Induction:*
  1. *Base Case:* Verify $P(1)$.
  2. *Hypothesis:* Assume $P(k)$ is true.
  3. *Inductive Step:* Show $P(k) \implies P(k+1)$ (or $P(k+2)$ depending on step size).
  4. *Conclusion:* True for all $n$ by PMI.
- *Inequality Induction:*
  - /Example:/ Proving $2^n < n!$ for $n \ge 4$.
  - /Technique:/ Manipulate one side to match the other: $2^{k+1} = 2 \cdot 2^k < 2 \cdot k! < (k+1)k!$.
- *Strong Induction:* Uses $\big(\bigwedge_{i=1}^k P(i)\big) \implies P(k+1)$.

* Set Theory & Functions
** Cardinality
- *Countable:* Finite sets, $\mathbb{N}, \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Q}$, finite subsets of $\mathbb{N}$ (e.g., finite bit strings), $\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}$.
- *Uncountable:* $\mathbb{R}, (0,1)$, infinite bit strings, functions $f: \mathbb{N} \to \{0,1\}$.
  - /Cantor-Schröder-Bernstein (CSB):/ If there exist injections $f: A \to B$ and $g: B \to A$, then $|A|=|B|$. Used to prove $|(0,1)| = |[0,1]|$.
- *Power Set:* If $|A| = n$, then $|\mathcal{P}(A)| = 2^n$.

** Function Properties
- *Injectivity (One-to-One):*
  - /Definition:/ $f(x) = f(y) \implies x = y$.
  - /Calculus Check:/ If $f'(x) > 0$ (strictly increasing), $f$ is injective.
- *Surjectivity (Onto):*
  - /Definition:/ For every $y \in \text{Codomain}$, $\exists x$ such that $f(x) = y$.
- *Composition:* If $g \circ f$ is injective, $f$ (inner) must be injective.
- *Inverse Images:*
  - Identity: $f^{-1}(A \cap B) = f^{-1}(A) \cap f^{-1}(B)$.
  - /Note:/ $f$ is injective $\iff f(A \cap B) = f(A) \cap f(B)$ for all subsets.

* Relations
** Properties
- *Reflexive:* $\forall x, (x,x) \in R$.
- *Symmetric:* $(x,y) \in R \implies (y,x) \in R$.
- *Transitive:* $(x,y) \in R \land (y,z) \in R \implies (x,z) \in R$.
- *Antisymmetric:* $(x,y) \in R \land (y,x) \in R \implies x = y$.

** Equivalence Relations
- Must be Reflexive, Symmetric, and Transitive.
- /Example:/ $x R y \iff 4 \mid (3x + y)$ is an equivalence relation.

* Number Theory
** Modular Arithmetic
- *Properties:*
  - If $a \equiv b \pmod m$, then $a^k \equiv b^k$ and $ac \equiv bc$.
  - *Zero Divisors:* Elements $a \ne 0$ where $ab \equiv 0 \pmod n$ (e.g., 4 in $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$).
  - *Units:* Elements coprime to $n$ have inverses (e.g., $\{1, 3, 7, 9\}$ in $\mathbb{Z}_{10}$).

** Solving Equations
- *Linear Congruences:* $ax \equiv b \pmod n$. Solvable if $\gcd(a,n) \mid b$.
- *Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT):*
  - System: $x \equiv a \pmod n, \quad x \equiv b \pmod m$ (where $\gcd(n,m)=1$).
  - /Method:/ Express $x = nk + a$, substitute into second equation.
- *Diophantine Impossibility:*
  - To prove no integer solutions for $15x^2 - 7y^2 = 9$:
  - Analyze modulo 3 or 5. Check quadratic residues ($x^2 \pmod 5 \in \{0, 1, 4\}$). Contradiction arises (e.g., needing $k^2 \equiv 2 \pmod 5$).

* Complex Numbers
** Forms & Powers
- *Representations:* Rectangular $z = a+bi$, Exponential $z = re^{i\theta}$.
- *De Moivre:* $(re^{i\theta})^n = r^n e^{in\theta}$.
- *Roots of Unity:* $z^n = 1$ forms a regular polygon.

** Geometry (Loci)
- *Circle:* $|z - a| = r$.
- *Perpendicular Bisector:* $|z - a| = |z - b|$.
- *Ellipse:* $|z - a| + |z - b| = k$ where $k > |a-b|$.
- *Apollonius Circle:* $|z - a| = k|z - b|$ ($k \ne 1$).
  - /Proof:/ Square both sides $|z-a|^2 = k^2|z-b|^2$ and expand using $z=x+iy$.

* Analysis (Sequences & Limits)
** Sequences
- *Boundedness:* Convergent $\implies$ Bounded.
- *Monotone Convergence:* A bounded, monotonic sequence converges.
- *Recursive Limits:*
  - Given $a_{n+1} = \sqrt{2 + a_n}$, prove bounded ($<2$) and increasing.
  - Find limit $L$: Solve $L = \sqrt{2+L} \implies L=2$.

** Limit Computations
- *Conjugate Trick:* For $\lim (\sqrt{n^2+5n} - n)$, multiply by $\frac{\sqrt{\dots}+n}{\sqrt{\dots}+n}$ to remove radical.
- *Factorials:* $\lim \frac{n!}{n^n} = 0$.

** Formal Proofs
- *Epsilon-N ($\epsilon-N$):*
  - Goal: $|a_n - L| < \epsilon$ for $n > N$.
  - Strategy: Bound term by $\frac{C}{n}$, set $\frac{C}{N} < \epsilon$.
- *Epsilon-Delta ($\epsilon-\delta$):*
  - Goal: $0 < |x-c| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \epsilon$.
  - /Example:/ $\lim_{x \to 2} x^2 = 4$. Decompose $|x^2-4| = |x-2||x+2|$. Bound $|x+2|$ by assuming $\delta \le 1$.

* Combinatorics
** Counting Techniques
- *Permutations:* $n!$ for distinct items.
- *Mississippi Rule:* For indistinguishable items: $\frac{n!}{n_1! n_2! \dots}$.
- *Stars and Bars:*
  - Integer solutions to $x_1 + \dots + x_k = n$ is $\binom{n+k-1}{k-1}$.
  - /Constraints:/ If $x_i \ge c$, substitution $y_i = x_i - c$ reduces $n$ by $\sum c$.

** Binomial Coefficients
- Coefficient of $x^k y^{n-k}$ in $(x+y)^n$ is $\binom{n}{k}$.

* Linear Algebra & Vector Spaces
** Vector Spaces
- *Subspaces:* Must contain $\mathbf{0}$, be closed under addition, and closed under scalar multiplication.
  - /Fails:/ Lines not through origin ($x+y=1$), Polynomials of degree *exactly* $n$.

** Vector Geometry
- *Dot Product:* $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0 \iff \mathbf{u} \perp \mathbf{v}$.
- *Projection:* $\text{proj}_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{v} = \frac{\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}}{|\mathbf{u}|^2}\mathbf{u}$.
- *Decomposition:* $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v}_{\parallel} + \mathbf{v}_{\perp}$.
- *Plane Equation:*
  - Normal $\mathbf{n} = \vec{AB} \times \vec{AC}$.
  - Equation: $\mathbf{n} \cdot (\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_0) = 0$.
- *Skew Lines Distance:* $D = \frac{|(\mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1) \cdot (\mathbf{d}_1 \times \mathbf{d}_2)|}{||\mathbf{d}_1 \times \mathbf{d}_2||}$.

* Polynomials & Rings
** Polynomial Properties
- *Vieta's Formulas:*
  - $\sum x_i = -a_{n-1}/a_n$.
  - $\sum x_i^2 = (\sum x_i)^2 - 2\sum x_i x_j$.
- *Remainder Theorem:* Remainder of $P(x) \div (x-c)$ is $P(c)$.
- *Conjugate Roots:* If coefficients are real, roots come in pairs $a \pm bi$.

** Ring Theory
- *Boolean Ring:* If $a^2 = a$ for all $a$, the ring is commutative ($ab = ba$).

* Computational (Julia)
- *Sets:* `Set([1, 2, 2])` results in `Set([1, 2])` (unique elements).
- *Control Flow:* `for x in S`, `if x % 2 != 0` (modulo check).
- *Modifying Collections:* `push!(collection, item)` appends data.
