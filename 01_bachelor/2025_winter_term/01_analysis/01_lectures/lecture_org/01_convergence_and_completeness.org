* 1. Convergence of Sequences (Metric Spaces)
** Metric Spaces (The Distance Interface)
*** [Definition 1.1.1: Metric]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Concept:** A "Metric" is an Interface specification for measuring "cost" or "distance" between two objects.
- **Input:** Two objects $x, y$ from a set $\Omega$.
- **Output:** A non-negative float representing distance.
- **Invariants (Unit Tests):**
  1.  **Identity:** Distance is 0 if and only if objects are identical ($x=y$).
  2.  **Symmetry:** Direction doesn't matter ($dist(x,y) = dist(y,x)$).
  3.  **Triangle Inequality:** The direct path is always the optimal (or equal) cost compared to a detour through $z$.
- **Edge Cases:** If $\Omega$ is empty, the definition is vacuously true.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- Think of a **Ruler** or a **GPS**.
- **Triangle Inequality ($d(x,z) \le d(x,y) + d(y,z)$):** This is the core physical intuition. If walking from Home to Work ($x \to z$) is 5km, walking Home to Coffee Shop to Work ($x \to y \to z$) cannot be *shorter* than 5km. It can only be longer or equal.
- Without a valid metric, we have no concept of "near" or "far," which means we cannot define limits.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
Given a set $\Omega \neq \emptyset$, a mapping $d_{\Omega}: \Omega \times \Omega \to [0, \infty)$ is a metric if:
1. **Symmetry:** $\forall x, y \in \Omega, d_{\Omega}(x, y) = d_{\Omega}(y, x)$
2. **Triangular Inequality:** $\forall x, y, z \in \Omega$,
   $$ d_{\Omega}(x, z) \le d_{\Omega}(x, y) + d_{\Omega}(y, z) $$
3. **Definiteness:** $\forall x, y \in \Omega$,
   $$ d_{\Omega}(x, y) = 0 \iff x = y $$

** Convergence of Sequences
*** [Definition 1.1.3: Convergence]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** To validate that a stream of data $(a_n)$ stabilizes at a final value $a$.
- **The Challenger (User):** Provides an arbitrary error tolerance $\epsilon > 0$ (e.g., "I need precision to $10^{-6}$").
- **The Responder (System):** Must find a timestamp $N$ (index).
- **The Guarantee:** For *all* times $n \ge N$, the error $|a_n - a|$ is strictly less than $\epsilon$.
- **Control Flow:**
  - Loop: `For all` $\epsilon$...
  - Action: `Find` $N$...
  - Check: `Verify` sequence stays inside bounds after $N$.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **The "Sniper" Analogy:**
  - The limit $a$ is the bullseye.
  - The sequence terms are shots fired.
  - To be "convergent" means no matter how tiny the target ring ($\epsilon$) is drawn, eventually ($N$) the shooter hits inside that ring **every single time** thereafter.
- **Key distinction:** It doesn't matter if the first million shots are wild. It only matters what happens closer to infinity (the "tail").

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
Let $(\Omega, d_{\Omega})$ be a metric space. A sequence $(a_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \subseteq \Omega$ converges to $a \in \Omega$ if:
$$ \forall \epsilon > 0, \exists N \in \mathbb{N} \text{ such that } \forall n \ge N: d_{\Omega}(a_n, a) \le \epsilon $$
Notation: $\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = a$ or $a_n \to a$.

*** [Theorem 1.1.5: Uniqueness of Limit]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** Verify system determinism.
- **Assertion:** A sequence cannot settle in two different states simultaneously.
- **Proof Logic (Contradiction):**
  - Assume it converges to both $a$ and $a'$.
  - Set tolerance $\epsilon$ to be less than half the distance between $a$ and $a'$.
  - The sequence would have to be in two disjoint bubbles at the same time. Impossible.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **"You can't be in two places at once."**
- If you are "very close" to New York and "very close" to London at the same time, New York and London must be the same place.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
If $a_n \to a$ and $a_n \to a'$, then $a = a'$.
*Proof Argument:*
$$ d_{\Omega}(a, a') \le d_{\Omega}(a, a_N) + d_{\Omega}(a_N, a') \le 2\epsilon $$
Since $\epsilon$ is arbitrary, $d_{\Omega}(a, a') = 0 \implies a = a'$.

* 1.2 Completeness
** Cauchy Sequences & Completeness
*** [Definition 1.2.1: Cauchy Sequence]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** Check for stability *without knowing the answer*.
- **Concept:** "Internal Consistency" test.
- **The Test:** Do the elements get closer to *each other* as time goes on?
- **Difference from Convergence:** Convergence checks $distance(a_n, \text{Target})$. Cauchy checks $distance(a_n, a_m)$.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **The "Huddle":** The sequence members are crowding together. They stop moving apart.
- **The Tragedy of the Rationals:** A sequence can huddle together perfectly (be Cauchy) but have nowhere to go.
  - *Example:* The sequence $1, 1.4, 1.41, 1.414...$ (approximating $\sqrt{2}$).
  - In $\mathbb{Q}$ (fractions), this sequence is trying to land on a "hole". It is Cauchy, but does not converge *in* $\mathbb{Q}$.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
A sequence $(a_n)$ is a Cauchy sequence if:
$$ \forall \epsilon > 0, \exists N \in \mathbb{N} \text{ such that } \forall n, m \ge N: d_{\Omega}(a_n, a_m) \le \epsilon $$

*** [Definition 1.2.5: Complete Metric Space]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** System Integrity Certification.
- **Definition:** A space is "Complete" if it has no "holes".
- **Rule:** `Strict Mode`. Every sequence that passes the "Internal Consistency Check" (Cauchy) must successfully return a valid limit object (Convergent).

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **Filling the Holes:** $\mathbb{R}$ (Real numbers) is just $\mathbb{Q}$ (Rationals) with all the holes filled in.
- If you walk towards a cliff edge in a Complete world, the ground effectively generates underneath your feet so you land on something.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
A metric space $\Omega$ is called **complete** if every Cauchy sequence in $\Omega$ is convergent in $\Omega$.
- **Axiom:** $\mathbb{R}$ is complete.
- **Lemma:** $\mathbb{Q}$ is NOT complete.

** Subsequences & Compactness
*** [Theorem 1.2.9: Bolzano-Weierstrass]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** Handle infinite data in finite memory.
- **Input:** A sequence that is **Bounded** (stuck in a box).
- **Output:** A subsequence that **Converges**.
- **Logic:** If you generate infinite points but limit the range of values (bounds), you are forced to revisit the same neighborhood infinitely often.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **Infinite Pigeonhole Principle:**
  - Imagine an infinite number of pigeons flying into a finite grid of cages.
  - At least one specific area must get crowded with infinite pigeons.
  - If we just track those specific pigeons, they converge to a specific spot.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
If $(a_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is a **bounded** sequence, then it contains a **convergent subsequence** $(a_{n_j})_{j \in \mathbb{N}}$.
