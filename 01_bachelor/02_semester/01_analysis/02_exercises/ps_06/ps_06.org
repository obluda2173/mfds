* Problem 1 :taylor_formula:multi_index:chain_rule:
** Task
#+begin_quote
( ... used in the proof of the Taylor formula)

(a) Suppose that $\alpha \in \mathbb{N}^n$ and $h \in \mathbb{R}^n$ such that $\|h\|_\infty \leq 1$. Prove that
\[ |h^\alpha| \leq \|h\|^{|\alpha|} \]

(b) Let $k \in \mathbb{N}$ and suppose that $f : \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ is $k$ times continuously differentiable. For $x, x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$ and $h = x - x_0$, define
\[ g : [-1, 1] \to \mathbb{R}, \quad g(t) := f(x_0 + th) \]
For $j \in \{0, \ldots, k\}$ prove that $g$ is $k$ times continuously differentiable and
\[ g^{(j)}(t) = \sum_{\substack{\alpha \in \mathbb{N}^n \\ |\alpha| = j}} \frac{j!}{\alpha!} (\partial^\alpha f)(x_0 + th)\, h^\alpha, \quad t \in [-1, 1] \]
(hint: induction and chain rule)
#+end_quote

* Errors/Typos
- Problem 1(a): "$\|h\|^{|\alpha|}$" → "$\|h\|_\infty^{|\alpha|}$" (missing $\infty$ subscript on RHS; the natural bound from $|h_i| \leq \|h\|_\infty$ is in $\|h\|_\infty$)
- Problem 1(b): "$x, x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$" → "$x, x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n$" (required since $h = x - x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n$)
- Problem 2(a): "$x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$" → "$x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n$"
- Problem 2(a): "$(\nabla f(x_0))^\top (x - x_0)^\alpha$" → "$(\nabla f(x_0))^\top (x - x_0)$" (extraneous $\alpha$ exponent; no multi-index $\alpha$ is defined in this problem)
