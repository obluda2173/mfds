* Problem 1.45 :dual_space:annihilator:basis:
** Task
#+begin_quote
(Dual space and annihilator)

Let $\mathbb{E} = \mathbb{R}_n[X]$ with $n \geq 1$, and let $f \in \mathbb{E}^*$ be defined by $f(p(X)) = p(0)$.

(i) Let $\mathbb{F} = \{p(X) \in \mathbb{E} : p(1) = 0\}$. Find a basis of $\mathbb{F}$. What is $\dim \mathbb{F}$?

(ii) Verify that $f \in \mathbb{F}^\circ$, is $f$ a basis for $\mathbb{F}^\circ$?
#+end_quote

** Solution

*** (i)

Let $p(X) = a_0 + a_1 X + \ldots + a_n X^n \in \mathbb{F}$. Then $p(1) = 0$ gives:

\begin{align*}
a_0 + a_1 + \ldots + a_n &= 0 \\
a_0 &= -(a_1 + \ldots + a_n)
\end{align*}

Substituting back:

\begin{align*}
p(X) &= -(a_1 + \ldots + a_n) + a_1 X + \ldots + a_n X^n \\
     &= a_1(X - 1) + a_2(X^2 - 1) + \ldots + a_n(X^n - 1)
\end{align*}

The coefficients $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in \mathbb{R}$ are free, so $\mathcal{B}_{\mathbb{F}} = \{X - 1,\; X^2 - 1,\; \ldots,\; X^n - 1\}$ spans $\mathbb{F}$.

These $n$ polynomials are linearly independent (the $k$ th has degree $k$), hence $\mathcal{B}_{\mathbb{F}}$ is a basis and $\dim \mathbb{F} = n$.

*** (ii)

$f \notin \mathbb{F}^{\circ}$: Take $X - 1 \in \mathbb{F}$. Then $f(X - 1) = (0) - 1 = -1 \neq 0$, so $f$ does not vanish on $\mathbb{F}$.

Since $f \notin \mathbb{F}^{\circ}$, $\{f\}$ is not a basis for $\mathbb{F}^{\circ}$.
