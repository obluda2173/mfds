* Problem 1.45                                         :dual_space:annihilator:basis:
** Task
#+begin_quote
(Dual space and annihilator)

Let $\mathbb{E} = \mathbb{R}_n[X]$ with $n \geq 1$, and let $f \in \mathbb{E}^*$ be defined by $f(p(X)) = p(0)$.

(i) Let $\mathbb{F} = \{p(X) \in \mathbb{E} : p(1) = 0\}$. Find a basis of $\mathbb{F}$. What is $\dim \mathbb{F}$?

(ii) Verify that $f \in \mathbb{F}^\circ$, is $f$ a basis for $\mathbb{F}^\circ$?
#+end_quote

* Problem 2.2                     :inner_product:completing_the_square:change_of_basis:orthonormal_basis:
** Task
#+begin_quote
(Lagrange's Method for obtaining orthonormal basis)

In $\mathbb{E} = \mathbb{R}^2$ we define the inner product
\[
\omega(x, y) = \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = x_1 y_1 + 2x_1 y_2 + 2x_2 y_1 + 3x_2 y_2
\]

(i) Let $\|\cdot\|$ be the norm associated to $\omega$, express $\|x\|^2$ in terms of the coordinates $x_i$ of $x = (x_1, x_2)^T$.

(ii) By completing the square on the expression obtained in the previous point, verify that $\|x\|^2 = (x_1 + 2x_2)^2 + x_2^2$.

(iii) Let $y = (y_1, y_2)$ where $y_1 = x_1 + 2x_2$ and $y_2 = x_2$. Write out the matrix equation $y = Qx$ that relates $y$ and $x$.

(iv) From the point of view of change of basis, the matrix $Q$ transforms the coordinates $(x_1, x_2)^T$ from the standard basis $\mathcal{E} = \{e_1, e_2\}$ to the coordinates $(y_1, y_2)^T$ of a new basis $\mathcal{U} = \{u_1, u_2\}$. Obtain the coordinates of $u_1$ and $u_2$ with respect to the standard basis $\mathcal{E}$.

(v) Verify that $\mathcal{U} = \{u_1, u_2\}$ is an orthonormal basis with respect to $\omega(\cdot, \cdot)$, that is, $\omega(u_i, u_j) = \delta_{ij}$.
#+end_quote
