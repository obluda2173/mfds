** Section C (Complex Open)

**C1 (Induction)**
- **Base:** $n=1 \Rightarrow 1 = \frac{1(2)(3)}{6} = 1$.
- **Hypothesis:** Assume $\sum_{1}^k = \frac{k(k+1)(2k+1)}{6}$.
- **Step:** Add $(k+1)^2$. Factor $\frac{k+1}{6}(k(2k+1) + 6(k+1)) = \frac{k+1}{6}(2k^2 + 7k + 6)$.
- Factor quadratic: $(2k+3)(k+2)$. Result matches $n=k+1$. *[2 pts]*

**C2 (Relations)**
- **Reflexive:** $x+x=2x$ (even).
- **Symmetric:** $x+y$ even $\implies y+x$ even.
- **Transitive:** $x+y=2k, y+z=2j$. Sum: $x+2y+z = 2(k+j) \implies x+z = 2(k+j-y)$ (even).
- **Classes:** Evens $[0]$ and Odds $[1]$. *[2 pts]*

**C3 (Limit Proof)**
- $|\frac{2n}{n+1} - 2| = |\frac{2n - 2n - 2}{n+1}| = \frac{2}{n+1}$.
- We need $\frac{2}{n+1} < \epsilon$.
- Choose $N > \frac{2}{\epsilon} - 1$.
- For all $n > N$, inequality holds. *[2 pts]*

**C4 (Sets)**
- **True.** De Morgan's Laws for sets.
- $x \in A \setminus (B \cap C) \iff x \in A \land x \notin (B \cap C)$.
- $\iff x \in A \land (x \notin B \lor x \notin C)$.
- $\iff (x \in A \land x \notin B) \lor (x \in A \land x \notin C)$.
- $\iff x \in (A \setminus B) \cup (A \setminus C)$. *[2 pts]*

**C5 (Combinatorics)**
- Divide triangle into 4 smaller equilateral triangles of side length 1 (connect midpoints).
- We have 5 points and 4 regions.
- By PHP, at least one region contains 2 points.
- Max distance in equilateral triangle of side 1 is 1. *[2 pts]*

**C6 (Complex)**
- $z^3 = 8e^{i(\pi/2 + 2\pi k)}$.
- Roots: $2e^{i(\pi/6 + 2\pi k/3)}$.
- $k=0: 2(\cos 30^\circ + i \sin 30^\circ) = \sqrt{3} + i$.
- $k=1: 2(\cos 150^\circ + i \sin 150^\circ) = -\sqrt{3} + i$.
- $k=2: 2(\cos 270^\circ + i \sin 270^\circ) = -2i$. *[2 pts]*

**C7 (Cardinality)**
- Map $f(n)$ as follows:
- Even $n=2k \to k$ (Positive).
- Odd $n=2k+1 \to -k$ (Negative).
- $f(0)=0, f(1)=-1, f(2)=1, f(3)=-2 \dots$ (Zig-zag mapping).
- This is a bijection. *[2 pts]*

**C8 (Number Theory)**
- $x \equiv 1 \pmod 3 \land x \equiv 2 \pmod 5 \implies x = 3k+1 \implies 3k+1 \equiv 2 (5) \implies 3k \equiv 1 \equiv 6 (5) \implies k \equiv 2 (5)$.
- $x = 3(5j+2) + 1 = 15j + 7$.
- $15j + 7 \equiv 3 \pmod 7 \implies 1j + 0 \equiv 3 (7) \implies j \equiv 3 (7)$.
- $x = 15(7m+3) + 7 = 105m + 45 + 7 = 105m + 52$.
- Solution: $52$. *[2 pts]*
