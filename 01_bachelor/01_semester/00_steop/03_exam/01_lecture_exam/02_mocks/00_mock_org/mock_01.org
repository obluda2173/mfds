** Section B
*** B1: Induction
- *Base Case* ($n=1$): LHS $\sum_{1}^1 (2(1)-1) = 1$, RHS $1^2 = 1$. âś“ (1 pt)
- *Hypothesis*: Assume $\sum_{i=1}^k (2i-1) = k^2$. (1 pt)
- *Step*:
  $\sum_{i=1}^{k+1} (2i-1) = k^2 + (2(k+1)-1) = k^2 + 2k + 1 = (k+1)^2$. (3 pts)
- *Conclusion*: Holds for all $n$. (1 pt)

*** B2: Functions
- *Injective*:
  $f(x)=f(y) \Rightarrow \frac{2x+1}{x-3} = \frac{2y+1}{y-3} \Rightarrow x=y$. (2 pts)
- *Surjective*:
  Solve $y=\frac{2x+1}{x-3}$ â†’ $x=\frac{3y+1}{y-2}$, valid for $y\neq2$. (2 pts)
- *Inverse*:
  $f^{-1}(x)=\frac{3x+1}{x-2}$. (2 pts)

*** B3: Relations
- *Reflexive*: $4 | (3x+x)$ â†’ $4|4x$. True. (1.5 pts)
- *Symmetric*: Assume $4 | (3x+y)$. Then $3x+y = 4x - (x-y)$ â‡’ $x-y$ divisible by 4 â‡’ $3y+x$ divisible by 4. (2.5 pts)
- *Transitive*:
  If $4|3x+y$ and $4|3y+z$, add: $3x+4y+z$ â†’ $3x+z$ divisible by 4. (2 pts)

*** B4: System of Congruences
- $x = 3k + 2$
- $3k+2 \equiv 3 \pmod{5}$ â†’ $3k \equiv 1$ â†’ $k = 2 \pmod 5$
- $k = 5j + 2$
- $x = 15j + 8$
- $15j+8 \equiv 2 \pmod 7$ â†’ $j \equiv 1 \pmod 7$
- $x = 105m + 23$
- *Smallest positive solution*: **23**. (6 pts)

*** B5: Combinatorics
1. Total ways: $\binom{14}{5} = 2002$. (2 pts)
2. Forbidden case: all CS â†’ $\binom{6}{5}=6$. (2 pts)
3. Result: $2002 - 6 = 1996$. (2 pts)

*** B6: Complex Numbers

- Roots: $z_k = 3 e^{i(3\pi/2 + 2\pi k)/3}$

(2 pts each)

*** B7: Polynomials
