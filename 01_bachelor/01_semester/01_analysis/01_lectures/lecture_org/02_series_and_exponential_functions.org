* 1.3 Series (Infinite Aggregation)
** The Definition of Series
*** [Definition: Infinite Sums as Partial Aggregates]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Concept:** An Infinite Series is not a static sum; it is a **Process**.
- **The Object:** We take a sequence $(a_n)$ (the inputs) and transform it into a sequence of **Partial Sums** $(S_N)$ (the accumulator).
- [cite_start]**The Check:** We test if the accumulator $S_N$ converges to a limit $S$ as $N \to \infty$[cite: 153, 155].
- **State Tracking:**
  - `Input[n]`: $a_n$
  - [cite_start]`Accumulator[N]`: $S_N = \sum_{n=0}^{N} a_n$ [cite: 152]
  - [cite_start]`Result`: Limit of `Accumulator`[cite: 155].

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **Stacking Bricks:**
  - Imagine stacking bricks of height $a_0, a_1, a_2...$.
  - $S_N$ is the total height of the tower after $N$ bricks.
  - "Convergence" means the tower stops growing and hits a specific ceiling height $S$, rather than growing infinitely tall.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
Given a sequence $(a_n)$, we define the partial sums $S_N := \sum_{n=0}^N a_n$. If $(S_N)$ converges, we write:
$$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n := \lim_{N \to \infty} \sum_{n=0}^N a_n $$
[cite_start][cite: 152, 155]

** Necessary Conditions & Geometric Series
*** [Lemma 1.3.1: The "Vanishing Terms" Sanity Check]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** Early rejection of divergent series.
- **The Rule:** If the input stream $(a_n)$ does not go to zero, the sum **cannot** converge.
- **Logic Flow:**
  - `IF` $\sum a_n$ converges `THEN` $a_n \to 0$.
  - **Crucial Warning (Common Bug):** The reverse is **FALSE**. Just because $a_n \to 0$ does not mean the sum converges (e.g., Harmonic Series).
  - **Logic:** If you keep adding non-zero sized bricks, the tower grows forever.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **The Leaky Faucet:**
  - If you are filling a bucket ($S_N$) with drops of water ($a_n$).
  - If the drops don't eventually become microscopic mist (approach 0), the bucket will overflow.
  - **Why it's not sufficient:** Even if the drops get smaller, if they don't get smaller *fast enough*, you still flood the room.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
If the sequence of partial sums $(S_N)$ converges, then:
$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0 $$
[cite_start][cite: 158]

*** [Theorem 1.3.2: The Geometric Series]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Concept:** The "Base Case" for almost all convergence tests.
- **Spec:** A series generated by a constant multiplier $x$.
- **Condition:** Converges `IFF` the multiplier's magnitude $|x| [cite_start]< 1$[cite: 168].
- **Closed Form:** We have an exact formula for the sum, not just an approximation.

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **Zeno's Paradox:**
  - Walk halfway to the wall (1/2), then half of what's left (1/4), then half again (1/8).
  - You are adding infinite steps, but you never walk further than the wall (Sum = 1).
  - If your step size multiplies by something $\ge 1$, you walk forever.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
For $|x| < 1$:
$$ \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} x^k = \frac{1}{1-x} $$
For partial sums ($x \ne 1$):
$$ \sum_{k=0}^{N} x^k = \frac{1 - x^{N+1}}{1-x} $$
[cite_start][cite: 165, 166]


** Convergence Algorithms (Tests)
*** [Theorem 1.3.5: The Test Suite]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Goal:** Determine system stability without calculating the exact final state.
- **1. Comparison Test:**
  - Logic: If `UnknownSeries` < `ConvergentSeries`, then `Unknown` converges.
  - [cite_start]Logic: If `UnknownSeries` > `DivergentSeries`, then `Unknown` crashes (diverges)[cite: 188].
- **2. Quotient (Ratio) Test:**
  - Logic: Compute the "decay rate" $\theta = |a_{n+1}/a_n|$.
  - [cite_start]Check: If $\theta < 1$, the series acts like a Geometric Series and converges[cite: 190].
  - Check: If $\theta > 1$, it diverges.
- **3. Leibniz (Alternating) Criterion:**
  - [cite_start]Logic: If terms flip sign $(+/-)$, get smaller, and hit 0, the oscillations dampen out and converge[cite: 193].

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **Comparison:** If I can fit my box inside your box, and your box fits in the room, my box fits in the room.
- **Ratio Test:** "How fast is the signal dying?" If the signal strength drops by 50% every second ($\theta=0.5$), it will eventually vanish.
- **Leibniz:** A "dampening spring." You jump forward 1 meter, back 0.5m, forward 0.25m. You are zeroing in on a location.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
1. **Comparison:** If $|a_n| [cite_start]\le |b_n|$ and $\sum |b_n|$ converges $\implies \sum |a_n|$ converges[cite: 188].
2. **Quotient:** If $\exists \theta \in (0,1)$ such that $|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}| [cite_start]\le \theta$, then $\sum |a_n|$ converges[cite: 190].
3. [cite_start]**Leibniz:** If $a_n \ge 0$ is decreasing and $a_n \to 0$, then $\sum (-1)^n a_n$ converges[cite: 193].


** The Exponential Function
*** [Definition 1.3.11: The "Master" Function]
**** Layer 1: The Logic & Spec View (QA Engineer)
- **Concept:** $e^x$ is defined not by geometry, but by a specific power series.
- **Stability:** Converges for **ALL** inputs $x$ (Radius of convergence is $\infty$).
- [cite_start]**Logic:** The $n!$ in the denominator grows so much faster than the power $x^n$ that it crushes the term size to zero very quickly[cite: 231].

**** Layer 2: The Feynman View (Intuition)
- **The Universal Translator:** This series turns **Addition** into **Multiplication** ($\exp(x+y) = \exp(x) \cdot \exp(y)$).
- **Euler's Number:** $e$ is just the output of this machine when you feed it $1$.

**** Layer 3: Formal Source of Truth
$$ \exp(x) := \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!} $$
[cite_start][cite: 228]
Functional Equation:
$$ \exp(x+y) = \exp(x) \cdot \exp(y) $$
[cite_start][cite: 251]
