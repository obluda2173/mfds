* Problem 2
Consider sets $X$ and $Y$ and a map $f: X\rightarrow Y$.
a. Let $B\subseteq Y$ be a subset. Define $f^{-1}(B)$. (1)
b. Define the term surjective for the map $f$. (1)
c. Is the map $f: \mathbb{R}_{+} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ with $f(x):=x^{4}$ surjective? ($\mathbb{R}_{ +}$ is the set of all non-negative real numbers) (1)
d. Define the identity map $x\mapsto x$. Also let us assume that there is a function $g: Y\rightarrow X$ such that $g\circ f = id_{X\rightarrow X}$ and $f\circ g = id_{Y\rightarrow Y}$. Prove that $g$ is unique. (2)

* Problem 5
a. Let $V$ be an $\mathbb{F}$ -vector space. Define the terms subspace, the sum of two subspaces and the terms direct sum and complement (of subspaces). (2)

c. Give a proof of the dimension theorem for subspaces. (6)

* Problem 6
a. Let $V$ be an $\mathbb{F}$ -vector space, and $h: V\rightarrow V$ a linear map. Is the set $H$ of all fixed points of $h$, i.e.,

   $H:=\{v\in V\mid h(v)=v\}$

   a subspace of $V$? (3)

b. Does the set $\mathbb{R}_{4}[X]$ of real polynomials of maximal degree four contain a spanning set of four elements? Provide an explanation for your answer. (1)
c. Is the following map $\mathbb{R}^{3}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}^{2}$ linear?

   $F(v_{1},v_{2},v_{3}):=\left\{\begin{array}{c}7v_{1}+3v_{2}-4v_{3}\\ v_{1}^{2}+2v_{2}-4v_{3}\end{array}\right$ ?

   Provide an explanation for your answer. (1)

d. Compute the kernel of the linear map $T: \mathbb{C}^{2}\rightarrow\mathbb{C}^{2}$

   $T(z,w):=\left(\begin{array}{c}(2+4i)z+2w\\ (5+6i)z+(3-1i)w\end{array}\right)$.

   Is the map injective? (3)
