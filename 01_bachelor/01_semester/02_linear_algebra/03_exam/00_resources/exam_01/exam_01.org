* Problem 1

* Problem 2
Consider sets $X$ and $Y$ and a map $f: X \rightarrow Y$.

(a) Let $A \subseteq X$ be a subset. Define $f(A)$. (1 Point)

(b) Define the term injective for the map $f$. (1 Point)

(c) Is the map $f: \mathbb{R}_+ \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ with $f(x) := x^4$ injective? ($\mathbb{R}_+$ is the set of all non-negative real numbers) (1 Point)

(d) Let $Z$ be another set and $g: Y \rightarrow Z$ another map. Define the composition of $f$ and $g$. (1 Point)

(e) Show that function composition is associative. (1 Point)

$\newpage$

* Problem 3
Compute all solutions of the following linear system of equations using LU factorization (if needed with partial pivoting). Explicitly provide the factors $L, U$, and the solution.

\begin{align*}
6x + 6y + 5z &= 1 \\
2x + 4y + 5z &= 2 \\
4x + 3y + 4z &= 1.
\end{align*}

(7 Points)

* Problem 4

A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 2 & 3 \\ 3 & 1 & 3 \\ 2 & 3 & 1 \end{pmatrix}.
$$
(6 Points)

* Problem 5

(b) Provide a proper formulation for the Steinitz exchange theorem (Theorem III.1.47). (2 Points)

(c) Give a proof of the Steinitz exchange theorem. (6 Points)

* Problem 6
(a) Show that the set $\{p \in \mathbb{R}[X] \mid p(0) = p(1) = 0\}$ is a subspace of the space of polynomials. (3 Points)

(b) Does the set $\mathbb{C}_5[X]$ of complex polynomials of maximal degree five contain a linearly independent set of seven elements? (Provide an explanation for your answer) (1 Point)

(c) Is the following set a subspace of the vector space $\mathbb{R}^3$?
$$
W := \{x \in \mathbb{R}^3 \mid x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 0\}?
$$
Provide an explanation for your answer. (1 Point)

(d) Compute the kernel of the linear map $T: \mathbb{C}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{C}^2$
$$
T(z, w) := \begin{pmatrix} (1 + 5i)z + 2w \\ (8 + i)z + (1 - 3i)w \end{pmatrix}.
$$
Is the map injective? (3 Points)
