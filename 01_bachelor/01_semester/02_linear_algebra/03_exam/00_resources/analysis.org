* DETAILED STYLE NOTES
- **Linear Maps (Theory):** Prof B consistently asks for 3 definitions (Kernel, Image, Rank) followed immediately by a major theorem statement and proof. In the *Target Exam*, this was the Steinitz Exchange Theorem. In *Historical Data*, it was often Rank-Nullity or Dimension Theorem for Sums.
- **Matrix Algebra:** The *Target Exam* specifically requested "LU factorization (with pivoting)" and "Inverse via elementary row operations". Historical papers focused heavily on modular arithmetic ($\mathbb{Z}_5, \mathbb{Z}_7$), whereas the Target Exam used $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$.
- **Vector Spaces:** Questions strictly require checking if a set is a subspace (polynomials or $\mathbb{R}^n$). The *Target Exam* included a specific check for a set defined by quadratic constraints ($x^2+...$), requiring a counter-example.
- **Maps & Fields:** The *Target Exam* devoted significant points (4pts) to the raw "Definition of a Field" and basic map properties (Injectivity/Composition), which is a shift from the purely calculation-heavy historical papers.
