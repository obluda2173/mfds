Exressions like x^2 - 3x or -7x^102 + (3 - i)x^17 - 7, or more generally,

        p(x) = a[n]x^n + a[n-1]x^n-1 + ... + a[1]x + a[0],

where coefficients a[0], a[1], ... a[n] are complex numbers, are called polynomials in x.

** Complex Conjugate Root Theorem
If polynomial has a real coefficients, then complex roots are always come in pairs - called conjugates.

- *What does it mean?*
  if
        (a + bi)

  is a root of polynomial, then

        (a - bi)

  is also a root

- *Example*
  Take a polynomial

        x^2 - 2x + 5 = 0

  Lets find its roots

        x = (2 ± sqrt{2^2 - 4 * 5})/(2)
          = (2 ± sqrt{-16})/(2)
          = 1 ± 2i

  Here the roots are 1 + 2i and 1 - 2i
