      - Analyze the "Past Paper Exam" to determine the **"Professor Style."** Look for specific phrasing habits, how trick questions are structured, the balance between theory and calculation, and the exact level of Julia code difficulty.

      - Identify which concepts appeared most frequently across seminars and lectures, ensuring the new exam covers high-priority topics.
   3. *Time Calibration & Structure:*
      - Total Time: 120 Minutes.
      - Structure: 10 MCQ (Part A) + 10 Short Open (Part B) + 8 Complex Open (Part C).

        - Part B: Short solutions solvable in under 5 minutes each.
        - Part C: Allocate remaining time for complex problem solving.
   4. *Difficulty Distribution:*
      - *Part A:* Basic conceptual understanding and quick calculations.
      - *Part B:* Standard seminar difficulty but with immediate/short solutions.

   5. *Mock Problems*
